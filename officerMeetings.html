Officer Meetings

Officer meetings are where you communicate with your officers and discuss upcoming events and make decisions for Student Council.

Getting Ready

In advance, you will need to make sure you know the topics to be discussed. Usually, over the weekend before the meeting, you will send out a form where officers can submit the topics that they would like to discuss at the upcoming meeting. Things you should ask for are:

  • The officer's name
  • The topic
  • Time needed to discus the topic
    • Use this to make sure all the topics fit in
  • Any information that does not require discussion
    • I introduced this idea this year, and it really helps by not spending time on information that could have been communicated earlier

If you don't have enough time to create a form, you can just have the officers email you the information. Both ways, make sure to let the officers know the agenda and the background information that was sent to you.

The actual meeting

Officer meetings are usually at 8:20, but if there is a lot to discuss it might be at 8:10. The meeting is usually in Rm. 33, but make sure to double check with an advisor at the beginning of the year in case the location has changed.

Officers should tell you in advance if they are not able to attend the meeting, but check your laptop (probably one of the class chromebooks just in case one of the officers just woke up sick.

Start the meeting by saying: I call this meeting to order. Today is an officer meeting on (insert date) at (insert time).

You are in charge now. It's your duty to direct the flow of discussion and make sure that the meeting doesn't get too off topic.

At the beginning of the meeting or after each topic, announce the next topic and how long it will be discussed. Make sure to set a timer (and move on the the next topic when it runs out) so that you can get through all the topics during the meeting.

One important thing to make sure of is that all the officers are being respectful of each other. Officer should not be packing up until the meeting is adjourned. Speaking of the meeting being adjourned...

To adjourn the meeting, simply say This meeting is adjourned. Thanks for coming! or something like that.
