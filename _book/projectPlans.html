Project Plans

As president, one of your duties is to approve project plans and keep track of progress of projects. Every project will have its own plan, covering all the details of the execution of the project.

Keeping track of approval

To have a project approved, it must be approved by these three people, in the order listed:

  1. Officer in Charge
  2. You (President)
  3. Advisor

So that you know what is the progress of approval, I would suggest creating a spreadsheet where project leaders update the status of their project.

Approving Projects

Make sure you have a system to have Leadership students notify you that their officer approval is done. I used a form.

When approving projects, make sure they have information such as the body of emails, announcements, and a detailed timeline done. Project plans should be formal, with proper grammar and spelling. One important part of project plans that students often forget about is having multiple backup plans. As your go through a project, think of what might go wrong, and if the project does not have a backup plan for that, put a comment.

After you have gone through the projects, if there are changes that need to be made set a due date for the next revision, otherwise put a comment stating that the project is approved.
